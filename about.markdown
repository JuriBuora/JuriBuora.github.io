# From Zero to Cybersecurity

This site documents my path into cybersecurity in public.

It is not a bootcamp landing page and not a claim that I am already an expert. It is a structured learning log where I document what I study, what I build, what I break, what I misunderstand, and what I eventually learn to explain properly.

## Goal

My objective is to become junior-level hireable in cybersecurity by building solid foundations and showing evidence of real practice.

I am focusing on:

- networking fundamentals
- Linux and command-line workflows
- web technologies and HTTP
- security concepts and attacker behavior
- SOC-style triage and detection thinking
- clear technical documentation

## Approach

I am trying to learn in a way that makes my thinking visible:

- hands-on labs instead of passive watching
- notes that explain the reasoning, not just the answer
- screenshots and command output when useful
- mistakes documented instead of hidden
- follow-up questions written down for later study

## Why public

Publishing the work helps me stay accountable and gives recruiters or mentors something concrete to review.

The point is not to look perfect. The point is to show progress, discipline, honesty, and the ability to learn from evidence.
